Julia analysis of the [Tidy Tuesday 2026-05-26](https://github.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday/tree/main/data/2026/2026-05-26) dataset: **Sustainable Energy for All** (SE4ALL). The data tracks country-level access to electricity, renewable energy shares by technology, and energy intensity from 1990–2010.

## Dataset

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Source | [SE4ALL Global Tracking Framework](https://www.se4all.org/) via TidyTuesday |
| Records | 5,271 country-year observations (228 countries) |
| Years | 1990–2010 |
| Key columns | Electricity access, renewable TFEC shares by technology, solar terajoules |
| Tools | Julia 1.12, CSV.jl, DataFrames.jl, Plots.jl |

The UN's SE4ALL initiative (2010) set three 2030 targets: universal energy access, doubled energy efficiency improvement, and doubled renewable share in the global energy mix. This dataset measures progress toward those goals through 2010.

## What to explore

The interactive explorer below computes statistics and charts live in the browser:

- **What this era reveals**: narrative insight cards backed by computed numbers
- **Global median trajectories**: three-panel view (access at reporting years, renewable TFEC with IQR band, solar/wind among adopters) plus narrative insights
- **Technology adoption wave**: stacked area of solar-only, both, and wind-only reporters, with growth stats and key findings
- **Biggest gainers**: dumbbell chart of who changed most (access, solar, wind, etc.)
- **Solar rank race**: bump chart of top-5 solar consumers shifting over time
- **At a glance**: headline stats (medians, adoption counts, outliers)
- **Renewable mix, scatter, heatmap, histogram**: composition, access vs renewables, adoption patterns
- **Which renewables grew fastest?**: ranked horizontal bars by technology with median comparison, country leader board, and key findings
- **Country trends & rankings**: line charts and solar terajoule rankings

## Reproducing the analysis

The Julia script loads the cleaned dataset, exports browser-ready JSON for the interactive charts, and writes static PNG fallbacks:

```julia
using CSV, DataFrames, Statistics, Plots

energy = CSV.read("research-docs/tidy-tuesday/data/energy_cleaned.csv", DataFrame; missingstring="NA")
export_explore_json(energy)  # writes static/tidy-tuesday/2026-05-26/explore.json
```

Run from the repository root:

```bash
julia research-docs/tidy-tuesday/analysis-2026-05-26.jl
```

Outputs land in `static/tidy-tuesday/2026-05-26/`.
